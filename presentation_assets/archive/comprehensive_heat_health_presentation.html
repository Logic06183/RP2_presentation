Geographically Weighted Principal Component Analysis

Figure 5. Spatial heterogeneity in heat-health vulnerability components. PC1 (Environmental Exposure): Northern industrial corridors show maximum exposure. PC2 (Health Vulnerability): Southern townships demonstrate concentrated health vulnerability requiring targeted interventions.

Environmental Exposure (PC1)

  • Northern regions: elevated exposure scores
  • Correlates with land surface temperature (r=0.73)
  • Urban heat island intensity drives patterns
  • Industrial activity amplifies exposure

Health Vulnerability (PC2)

  • Southern townships: concentrated vulnerability
  • Socioeconomic constraints limit adaptation
  • Healthcare access limitations
  • Population density amplifies risk

Statistical Spatial Validation (LISA)

Figure 7. Local Indicators of Spatial Association confirming statistically significant spatial clustering (Global Moran's I p < 2e-16). High-high vulnerability clusters concentrated in southern townships, enabling geographically clustered intervention strategies.

Statistical Validation

  • Global Moran's I: p < 2e-16 (highly significant)
  • Local Clustering: 89% high-vulnerability areas autocorrelated
  • Robustness: Consistent across spatial weight matrices
  • Significance: p < 0.05 for identified clusters

Implementation Implications

Clustered Interventions
Spatial clustering enables efficient intervention design through geographic proximity, reducing implementation costs while maximizing vulnerable population coverage.

Executive Policy Dashboard

Figure 10. Executive summary for evidence-based policy development. Key metrics enable targeted intervention strategies: early warning systems, cooling infrastructure, healthcare capacity building, and heat-resilient urban planning investments.
Evidence-Based Policy Recommendations
  • Johannesburg: Implement dry heat early warning systems (>35°C threshold) with healthcare surge capacity
  • Abidjan: Prioritize humidity-heat stress monitoring and expanded cooling infrastructure access
  • Both Cities: Strengthen healthcare capacity during peak heat seasons with vulnerable population targeting
  • Investment Priority: Heat-resilient urban planning, green infrastructure, and cooling centers
  • Data Systems: Real-time heat-health surveillance networks with predictive capabilities
  • Cross-Climate Learning: Adaptation strategies transferable across African urban contexts
